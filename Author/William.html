William Shakespeare

William Shakespeare (1564–1616) was an English playwright, poet, and actor, widely regarded as the greatest writer in the English language and the world’s most famous dramatist. Known as the “Bard of Avon,” his works have shaped literature, theater, and culture for centuries.

He wrote 39 plays, 154 sonnets, and several narrative poems. His plays explore timeless themes of love, power, betrayal, and the human condition — blending deep emotion with poetic brilliance. Shakespeare’s influence continues to resonate through language, art, and performance across the world.
