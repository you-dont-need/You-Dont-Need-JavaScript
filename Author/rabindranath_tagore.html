Rabindranath Tagore

Rabindranath Tagore (1861–1941) was a Bengali poet, writer, composer, philosopher, and painter. He reshaped Bengali literature and music with his modernist and humanist vision. In 1913, he became the first non-European to win the Nobel Prize in Literature for his collection Gitanjali (Song Offerings). Tagore’s influence extended far beyond poetry — he was also an education reformer and a key figure in the Indian cultural renaissance.

His works reflect deep spiritual insight and universal humanism. He founded Visva-Bharati University in Santiniketan, aiming to blend Indian and Western traditions of education. His compositions include the national anthems of both India ("Jana Gana Mana") and Bangladesh ("Amar Shonar Bangla").
